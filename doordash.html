How we started

Since the outbreak of COVID-19, a lot of consumers have started to choose DoorDash and similar food delivery services. Their “no-contact” delivery service helped with the pandemic situation to make users feel more secure while accessing the wide variety of food in local restaurants. Generally, users can use Doordash to order foods from local restaurants, buy groceries online, and they even provide retail shopping services. As there are more users choosing the online delivery service, our team is interested in discovering the problems in the user experience of using such mobile apps; we chose Doordash specifically, because we want to improve it based on our personal experience compared to its other competitors.

Story 1

One of our teammates was very disappointed with a dinner order. It was 8 PM in the evening and she’s waiting for the dasher to deliver her order and the dasher called her to pick up the food. However, the dasher gave the food to the wrong person and left. The dinner was worth 70 dollars, and she had to contact customer service. The customer service was not able to contact the dasher. In the end, the Doordash customer service had to reorder the same food items again for her and she waited another hour.

Story 2

I once used the grocery shopping feature of DoorDash and I ordered many heavy groceries. Consider the weight of the order, I gave a lot tip to the dasher. However, due to the faliure of the app, the dasher was not able to replace items that were out of stock. It turned out only a few groceries were delivered to me and I regret for that amount of tip.

How's our peers' experience with DoorDash?

Due to the short amount of time we have for this project and pandemic, the primary source of data we collected are all from our peers who are aged around 18-25. Most of these users don’t use DoorDash very often but have used it regardless. Most users described using DoorDash for specifically food delivery services and a small amount for grocery service. We found that 25% of our survey respondents described encountering in-app issues when utilizing DoorDash. We specified in-app issues as being ‘lack of function’ and ‘confusion’. We gave respondents a place to describe these issues in the next question and we found that many had ‘app crashes’ and “promo offer bugs” while using DoorDash. When asking about dasher experiences, some users experienced wrong deliveries, incorrect orders, and missing items. Although some of these issues are system issues, there are ways to facilitate that these issues don’t happen through the app.

Problem Satement:

How might we give DoorDash users more control of their food’s delivery process by improving the dasher’s to user communication to make their food delivery service accurate?

User Flow

UI Sketch

After-service

Starting from screen 5, when dasher confirms the order delivery on their app, user will receive a notification saying that the dasher has confirmed the delivery and also asks if the user has received the food. If the user hit “YES,” they will be directed to screen 6 to rate the restaurant and dasher, or report any issues they’ve met; they’re also offered a second chance to tip the dasher if they feel like tipping the dasher again. If the user didn’t receive the food, they will be directed to screen 8, the dasher’s number will be provided again for users to call and check order status. At this point, if they cannot contact the dasher, they can directly call customer service immediately in the same page. This check-in screens may reduce users’ frustrations when they find out they haven’t received their food but the dasher already confirmed delivery; it will also help users to thank their dashers after the delivery and dashers could be potentially rewarded more tips from after-tipping functionality.

Rating

These sketches show more user centered after delivery process. The user is able to describe whether something went wrong and is able to confirm they have received the order, it does not rely solely on the dasher.

Low-Fi Prototype

Version A

This is our first prototype. It starts off with the order summary/progress and there’s options to call and text dasher and an option state when you receive your order. After you click “order received”, it prompts you to an after experience questionnaire asking your experience on order and dasher, a space to give an additional tip, and if there were any issues with their order. Each of the other screens pertain to the issues that the user experienced. This resolves some communication issues because there is a double confirmation barrier where order delivery is not only one sided towards the driver, it is now also the user confirming that everything went smoothly. If it did not go smoothly, the user is able to communicate which aspect did not go well.

Version B

This prototype focuses on having a double check window for the user when the dasher delivers the food. This is designed to prevent the errors or any mistakes the dasher may have, such as delivering the food to another place, the users can call them right away instead of going back and forth with customer service. Compared to the first prototype, this prototype focuses on the delivery process more instead of rating. As you can see in order page 2 and 5, users can declare if they have physically received the food after the dasher delivers the food. If they choose “yes,” they will be directed to the the rating page and they get to choose to tip the dashers after the service ends. The goal of this prototype is to make users more comfortable with knowing where the food is at. As in our user research, there are examples of dashers sending the food to somewhere else or the food is never delivered. The check-in window (order page 2) should make users to get their food much more quickly and it prevents the Dasher’s mistakes.

First Round User Testing

We invite 4 volunteers to experience through 2 prototypes and complete tasks we designed to test the functionality of the prototypes.

What went well

  • Users liked the “order confirmation” feature and they think it’s really helpful
  • Users also resonated with the “tipping after” feature, as they think it makes more sense to tip after the dasher delivers the food.
  • They all think the prototypes perform a better job compared to original Doordash in after service flows
  • The order overview with car information is helpful.
  • More detailed branch while rating the order.

What need to be improved

  • We could combine the good flow of prototype A and the innovative features of Prototype B
  • Refine the flow of prototype B since one user is confused about the location of pop up delivery confirmation.
  • Refine the UI, make buttons larger and the chat bubble more obvious.

Refined Prototype

For our new prototype we combined our first and second prototype and also changed a few features. Now, when a user indicates that they haven’t received their order, they will be shown the map of the dashers location where they indicated it’s been delivered (labeled as No- 1) along with the contact information and customer service. If they end up finding the order and/or confirm their order, they are directed to the survey. This survey could be after they realize something is wrong with the order just in case they didn’t get to tell the Dasher when they had the opportunity. We decided to get rid of the “service” issue due to a lot of confusion in our first round of user testing.

Rating(Alternative)

This alternative version allows users to provide immediate feedback after receiving the order. Help & Customer Service

Second Round User Testing

What went well

  • They liked the alternative way of separating restaurant and dasher ratings and comments
  • They liked that there was an option for positive comments and not just reporting issues

Issues

  • The wording “Order Experience” on the rating screen was not clear or specific enough to know what exactly they were rating.
  • The alternative was a bit more personal with the dasher picture and name
  • they are rating and tipping their experience before they are able to report issues. This can cause the user to be biased and not give good ratings
  • UI Style not following original design system
  • Rating page too long

Solution

Taking in all this feedback, we overall decided to combine our original and alternative rating screen (seen in first before and after). We took out the “report issues” section and integrated it into their own respective section within their rating screen. To do that, we had to have different screens for both restaurant rating and dasher rating. In addition, some users liked how the alternative had sections for positive feedback that the original did not have, so we added spots where users are able to express what went right. Similar to the DoorDash app at the moment, when a user rates the dasher, different options and feedback will appear depending on the amount of stars.
